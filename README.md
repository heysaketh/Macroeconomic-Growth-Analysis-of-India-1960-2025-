# Macroeconomic Growth Analysis of India (1960-2025)
Macroeconomic data analysis of India's growth dynamics using Excel, Power Query and Power BI.

## Overview

This project is a data-driven macroeconomic analysis of India's long-term growth dynamics using national accounts growth rate data. The study examines how private consumption, government expenditure, investment, and external demand have influenced GDP growth across multiple decades.

The dataset was cleaned, restructured, and modelled using Excel and Power Query. An interactive dashboard was developed in Power BI to visualise structural growth trends, demand cycles, nominal-real divergence, and macroeconomic volatility.

---

## Objectives

- Understand the structural drivers of India’s economic growth  
- Compare real vs nominal growth behaviour across time  
- Identify consumption-led vs investment-led growth phases  
- Analyse external sector volatility and growth shocks  
- Study long-term macroeconomic growth regimes  

---

## Dataset

The analysis uses national accounts growth rate data covering:

- Private Final Consumption Expenditure (PFCE)  
- Government Final Consumption Expenditure (GFCE)  
- Gross Fixed Capital Formation (GFCF)  
- Exports and Imports  
- GDP Growth  

The dataset was transformed from wide report format into analytical long-format suitable for dashboard modelling.

---

## Tools & Techniques

- **Microsoft Excel** → Data cleaning, restructuring, exploratory analysis  
- **Power Query** → Data transformation and unpivot modelling  
- **Power BI** → Interactive dashboard development and visual analytics  

---

## Dashboard Features

- Comparison of consumption, investment, and GDP growth cycles  
- Real vs nominal growth divergence analysis  
- Identification of high-growth and slowdown regimes  
- External sector growth trend visualisation  
- Long-term macroeconomic volatility patterns  

---

## Key Insights

- Consumption growth exhibits relatively lower volatility compared to investment growth  
- Investment cycles strongly influence periods of high GDP expansion  
- Nominal growth divergence increases during inflationary phases  
- External demand shocks contribute to short-term growth fluctuations  
- India’s growth pattern shows structural transitions across decades
